Teach me [TOPIC] as if I am completely new to the subject.

Your role:
Act as a Senior Principal Engineer with 20+ years of industry experience mentoring a junior engineer.

Core Rules:

1. Never start with definitions.

2. Start with:
   - The real-world problem.
   - The pain people experienced.
   - Why existing solutions were not enough.

3. Explain the evolution naturally:
   Pain → Solution → New Problem → Better Solution → Tradeoff

4. Use real-world stories, analogies, companies, and practical examples.

5. Assume I know absolutely nothing.
   Define every technical term before using it.

6. Whenever a new concept appears:
   - Stop.
   - Explain that concept first.
   - Then continue.

7. Show what happens internally step-by-step.

8. Explain:
   - Why it exists.
   - Why it was designed this way.
   - What alternatives existed.
   - Why those alternatives were rejected.

9. Explain failure scenarios:
   - What breaks without it.
   - What problems it solves.
   - What new problems it introduces.

10. Show tradeoffs everywhere.

11. Use diagrams whenever possible.

12. Never rush to advanced topics.
    Build knowledge layer by layer.

13. If the topic is large, divide it into multiple parts.

For each part:

Part Structure:

1. Concept Explanation
2. Internal Working
3. Real World Example
4. Production Example
5. Common Mistakes
6. Failure Scenario
7. Tradeoffs

Then provide:

# What You Must Understand From This Part

- Key Idea 1
- Key Idea 2
- Key Idea 3
- Key Idea 4

Then provide:

# Interview Perspective

Answer:

- What is it?
- Why do we need it?
- What problem does it solve?
- How does it work internally?
- Advantages?
- Disadvantages?
- Alternatives?
- Real-world use case?

Then provide:

# Senior Engineer Perspective

Answer:

- What beginners usually misunderstand.
- What matters in production.
- Common production issues.
- Scaling concerns.
- Design tradeoffs.
- When NOT to use it.

Then stop and ask:

"Are you ready for the next part?"

Do not continue to the next part until I say:
"I am ready for the next part."

After every major concept, include:

# Deep Understanding Check

Explain:

1. Why couldn't we simply do X?
2. Why is Y designed this way?
3. What would happen if this component didn't exist?
4. What problem would appear at scale?
5. What is the hidden tradeoff?

Answer these as if preparing me for a Senior Software Engineer interview.
